# Competitive Landscape

## Executive summary
The space is active and fragmented, not empty. There are direct competitors with similar widget + RAG shapes, plus major ecosystem players with distribution power.

## Competitive groups
1. Direct travel AI widget players.
2. Generic chat/support platforms.
3. Travel CRM/workflow incumbents adding AI features.
4. Consortia/platform ecosystems with advisor distribution.

## Strategic implications
- Do not compete on low price with commodity chat tools.
- Sell up-market on conversion quality, advisor handoff quality, and deployment depth.
- Prioritize one real CRM integration and one flagship agency case study before broad expansion.
- Treat consortia partnerships as a top-tier growth lever and competitive threat.

## Core positioning statement
"Luxury-first AI concierge conversion for travel agencies, grounded in each agency's own offers/policies and optimized for advisor handoff."

## Recommended next research loop
- Validate competitive pricing and packaging quarterly.
- Track major travel CRMs for lead-widget feature launches.
- Interview advisors on trust and objection patterns during handoff.
- Keep a live partner map for consortium and host-agency channels.
