# Go-To-Market and Viability

## VC-style take
This is a credible wedge, not a guaranteed company yet.

## Why it can work
- Large and growing luxury travel spend.
- Agencies need better inbound conversion and lead qualification.
- AI + human handoff model aligns with advisor-led selling behavior.

## Main risks
- Distribution risk: agency/channel access is harder than building the tech.
- Trust risk: advisors may resist AI touching high-value relationships.
- Moat risk: orchestration patterns are copyable; data/workflow depth is the real moat.

## Where defensibility comes from
- Embedded workflow depth in agency operations.
- Real CRM and data integrations that improve outcomes over time.
- Demonstrated conversion lift and booking impact, not generic chatbot utility.

## What to prove in pilots
- Lead capture lift versus existing site forms/chat.
- Qualification completeness and handoff usability for advisors.
- Measurable movement in meeting/booked-trip downstream metrics.

## Pilot go/no-go indicators
Go if:
- At least 2 of first 5 pilots show strong renewal intent.
- Advisors report handoff quality as net-positive.
- Funnel metrics show meaningful qualification and follow-up uplift.

Pause/reframe if:
- Low advisor trust persists despite onboarding.
- Deployment friction is high per tenant.
- Outcome lift is not measurable within 60-90 days.
